Loft Conversions

Dormer, hip-to-gable and mansard conversions, from head-height checks to building regulations sign-off.

What this covers

A loft conversion turns dead volume into a bedroom and bathroom, and its feasibility comes down to numbers: available head height, stair position and the permitted development volume allowance of 40 cubic metres for terraces and 50 for other houses.

We measure first, design second, and tell you honestly if the numbers do not work. Fees are fixed and confirmed in writing before each stage, and the first consultation is free.

What you receive

  • Head height and stair feasibility check
  • Dormer, hip-to-gable or mansard design
  • PD confirmation or planning application
  • Structural design and building regulations package

Why it matters

In conservation areas most loft PD rights are restricted and mansards typically need planning permission. Knowing this before design keeps months out of the programme.

Common questionsRead the full guide
Does a loft conversion need planning permission? +
Frequently not: rear dormers within the volume limits fall under permitted development. Front dormers, mansards and conservation-area lofts usually need an application. We check your property's planning history, conservation status and permitted development position as part of the free consultation.
What head height do I need? +
Around 2.2 metres at the ridge is the practical threshold for a comfortable conversion. We measure and confirm this at the first visit.

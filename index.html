Why clients choose FADP

Fixed fees, in writing

Fees are agreed and confirmed in writing before each work stage begins. No hourly surprises, no scope creep.

One architect throughout

The architect you meet at the first consultation designs your building and administers its construction. No handoffs.

Planning, handled

We prepare and submit your planning application, deal with the council, and manage conditions through to discharge.

Leave at any stage

Our appointments are broken into clear stages. You can conclude the engagement at the end of any stage, without penalty.

How it works
Step 1

Free consultation

A 30-minute conversation at the studio, on site or by phone, about your brief, site and budget. We'll tell you honestly whether it's a project we can help with, and what it's likely to cost.

Step 2

Design & planning

Measured survey, concept design and design development, then a planning application where one is needed. You approve the design before anything is submitted.

Step 3

Technical design

Building regulations drawings, structural coordination and a tender package that lets contractors price accurately, which protects your budget on site.

Step 4

Construction

We help you choose a contractor, administer the building contract, and inspect the work regularly through to completion and snagging.

Not sure where your project sits? Most enquiries start exactly there. The first consultation is for working that out.

Common questions
How much does an architect cost? +
It depends on the scope, but you'll know before you commit: we quote a fixed fee for each work stage in writing, after the free consultation. For a typical London extension, architectural fees usually fall between a defined percentage of build cost or a fixed sum. We'll set out both options and you choose.
Do I need planning permission? +
Not always. Many extensions and loft conversions fall under permitted development. Assessing this is one of the first things we do, and it's covered in the free consultation. Where an application is needed, we prepare and submit it for you.
How long will my project take? +
As a guide: design and planning typically take 3 to 5 months (councils have an 8-week statutory determination period), technical design 4 to 8 weeks, and construction from 3 months for an extension to a year or more for a new house. We'll give you a written programme for your specific project.
Can you recommend builders? +
Yes. We run a competitive tender with contractors we've worked with before, alongside any you'd like to include. You see all the prices, and the decision is yours.
What if I only want drawings, not the full service? +
That's fine. Because our appointments are broken into clear stages, you can engage us for planning drawings only, or up to tender, and stop at the end of any stage without penalty.
Do you work outside London? +
Most of our work is in London and the South East, but we take projects further afield where the brief is right, so ask us.
